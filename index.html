Abstract

We prove that neural model honesty decomposes into exactly two components — an information component (H-axis), detectable without labels from the model's internal geometry, and a magnitude component (G-axis), requiring external grounding — and that no single-model reference-free signal can supply both. This resolves a fundamental open question in hallucination detection: why reference-free detectors rank errors reliably yet fail to calibrate on severity, and what, precisely, external verification buys.

We establish four results. (1) The Conservation Law: a model's attributions are faithful if and only if its forward map is information-preserving (\(\mathcal{L}(f) = \mathbb{E}[|\log|\det J(x)||] = 0\)), verified to \(<7 \times 10^{-11}\) nats error across six contraction rates. The two-directional extension catches fabrication lies (measured: \(1.0986\) nats, predicted: \(1.0986\)) that the one-directional functional misses entirely. The quantum no-hiding theorem enforces this physically: \(S_{\mathrm{sys}} = S_{\mathrm{env}}\), gap \(= 1.01 \times 10^{-13}\). (2) The Gauge Correction: \(|\det J| = 1\) is necessary but not sufficient — a gauge-twin achieves \(\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{det}} = 4.4 \times 10^{-16}\) while producing a \(16.89\times\) attribution swing; the attribution-lie functional \(\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{attr}} = \sum|\log \sigma_i|\) catches it at \(5.163\) nats, and the additive diagonal repair restores uniqueness to \(9.2 \times 10^{-17}\). (3) The NTK Identity: training-support concentration equals NTK-GP posterior variance, verified to \(3.27 \times 10^{-13}\) over \(n = 300\) networks, AUC \(= 0.859 \pm 0.084\). (4) The Detection Ceiling: \(\partial v / \partial A = 0\) — flag-rate is locked constant while confabulation precision spans \(0.000 \to 0.928\) for v_ntk (and \(0.000 \to 0.078\) for a_kl), confirmed across four architectures and data regimes including a 117M-parameter transformer.

The synthesis: NTK-GP posterior variance is the H-axis; grounding residual is the G-axis; H + G is the minimal complete certificate. This derivation explains the architecture of frontier AI verification pipelines and proves that single-model confidence routing is miscalibrated for in-distribution errors by an irreducible amount.

Keywords: hallucination detection, neural tangent kernel, information geometry, conservation laws, faithfulness, calibration, AI safety

1Introduction

When a neural model answers a question, generates an attribution, or routes a decision, two fundamentally distinct forms of dishonesty are possible.

The first is structural dishonesty: the model's forward map destroys or invents information. Input features vanish from the output, or structure appears with no grounding in the input. This is a violation of an information conservation law and, as we show, is detectable from the model's internal geometry alone.

The second is magnitude dishonesty: the model fills a gap in its training distribution with a confident confabulation. The existence of such a gap is geometrically detectable via the NTK posterior variance. But how wrong the confabulation is cannot be estimated by any internal signal — the model was never trained on the ground truth. This is a provable detection ceiling, not an engineering limitation.

These two forms require two different mathematical tools. This paper derives both from first principles and proves they are jointly necessary and sufficient.

The two threads unified. This work combines two independent lines of investigation that turn out to be the same theorem seen from opposite directions: (a) Faithfulness as a Conservation Law — the structural certificate; and (b) Gaps, Not Lies: The Neural-Tangent Ceiling — why no single-model reference-free detector can calibrate for the hardest class of errors. The gauge-twin attack (§4) is the bridge: it shows a network can pass every conservation test while producing attribution lies, which forces the second functional, which reveals the NTK identity as its solution.

Central Theorem (Informal)

No single-model, reference-free signal can be calibrated for in-distribution confabulation. The best internal signal is the NTK–GP posterior variance, which detects gaps (exposure to a data region) but cannot detect lies (confabulation magnitude within that gap). Magnitude information is available only via external grounding.

Why this matters for deployed systems. Production pipelines deploying GPT-class models routinely use single-model confidence scores to route between inference tiers. Our theorem proves this routing signal is calibrated for out-of-distribution inputs and systematically miscalibrated for in-distribution gaps — and we derive exactly what an external verifier call provides.

2Background and Related Work

2.1 Information Conservation and Faithfulness

The Jacobian determinant \(\det J(x) = \det \frac{\partial f(x)}{\partial x}\) measures local volume change under the forward map. \(|\det J| < 1\) implies information deletion; \(|\det J| > 1\) implies information fabrication; \(|\det J| = 1\) is the conservation condition. Earlier work (Koh & Liang, 2017; Lundberg & Lee, 2017) proposed influence functions and SHAP as attribution certificates without addressing whether the attribution itself is faithful. We provide the missing conservation-law foundation.

2.2 Landauer's Principle (1961)

Erasing one bit costs at least \(k_B T \ln 2 \approx 2.85 \times 10^{-21}\) J at room temperature. Our lie functional in nats gives the physical energy lower bound: \(E_{\min} = k_B T \cdot \mathcal{L}(f)\) joules per inference — a thermodynamic lower bound on the cost of dishonesty.

2.3 Quantum No-Hiding (Braunstein & Pati, 2007)

Information deleted from a quantum system cannot vanish: it must appear in the environment, with \(S(\rho_S) = S(\rho_E)\) where \(S(\cdot)\) is von Neumann entropy. This enables lie detection without inspecting model output directly and provides an entropy-based physical grounding for our conservation law.

2.4 Neural Tangent Kernel (Jacot et al., 2018)

In the linearized-network regime, \(K_{\mathrm{NTK}}(x, x') = \nabla_\theta f(x)^\top \nabla_\theta f(x')\). The NTK–GP posterior variance, \[ \mathrm{Var}_{\mathrm{NTK}}(x) = k(x,x) - k(x,X)\,K^{-1}\,k(X,x), \] quantifies epistemic uncertainty about the network's output. We show this quantity equals training-support concentration exactly, making it the theoretical maximum-information internal signal.

2.5 Key Comparators

Attention-KL (van Dijk, 2026; arXiv:2605.05025) — current SOTA single-pass reference-free detector. We prove its calibration ceiling in §6–7.

Semantic entropy (Farquhar et al., 2024; Nature) — multi-sample signal. Escapes the single-pass ceiling by accessing magnitude information via inter-sample variance; discussed in §12.

Deep ensembles (Lakshminarayanan et al., 2017) — achieves 94.9% recall on independent in-distribution errors; requires ≥2 models; fails on shared-bias errors (§9).

Newman (1928) / Putnam — classical non-identifiability: internal structure cannot pin external meaning. We give the quantitative, magnitude-resolved ML instantiation.

3Experimental Setup

3.1 Synthetic Network Ensemble

All synthetic experiments use an ensemble of \(n = 300\) independent fully-connected networks with architecture \([d_{\rm in}, 64, 64, d_{\rm out}]\), ReLU activations, and Xavier initialization. Input dimension \(d_{\rm in} = 1\) for the conservation law sweep and \(d_{\rm in} = 11\) for the real-data (UCI wine) experiment; output dimension \(d_{\rm out} = 1\) throughout. Networks are trained on \(N = 100\) synthetic training points drawn i.i.d. from \(\mathcal{N}(0, I)\) for 500 steps with Adam (\(\eta = 10^{-3}\)). The NTK is computed in the infinite-width linearization via the empirical kernel \(K_{\mathrm{NTK}}(x,x') = \nabla_\theta f(x)^\top \nabla_\theta f(x')\), evaluated at the trained parameters. All 300 networks are independent — separate random seeds, separate parameter initializations — to ensure independent measurement of the NTK identity error.

3.2 Contraction Experiments

The thermodynamic sweep uses a scalar contraction map \(f_\alpha(x) = \alpha x\) for \(\alpha \in \{1.00, 0.90, 0.70, 0.50, 0.25, 0.10\}\). These are deterministic analytic functions, not trained networks; the Jacobian is \(J = \alpha\) (scalar), giving \(|\det J| = \alpha\) and \(\mathcal{L}(f_\alpha) = -\log \alpha\) exactly. The measured vs. predicted comparison in Table 1 therefore tests the implementation of the lie functional, not network training.

3.3 Confabulation Magnitude Parameter

Throughout §§5–7, confabulation is injected by shifting test-point labels by a fixed signed offset \(A\): for an honest test point \((x, y)\), the confabulated version is \((x, y + A)\). The model is never retrained after injection. The flag-rate is then \(v(\tau, A) = \Pr[\phi(x) > \tau]\), which by construction cannot depend on \(A\) because \(\phi\) depends only on the model and \(x\), not on \(y\). The A-sweep runs \(A \in \{0, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, 4.0, 8.0\}\) for synthetic networks and \(A \in \{0, 1, 3, 5, 8, 12, 18\}\) for the GPT-2 transformer.

3.4 Software and Reproducibility

All experiments are implemented in Python 3.11 with NumPy 1.26, SciPy 1.11, scikit-learn 1.3, and PyTorch 2.2. The quantum no-hiding experiment uses density-matrix arithmetic in exact NumPy (float64; no QPU). The transformer experiment uses GPT-2 (117M parameters) from HuggingFace Transformers 4.39. Every experiment produces a single JSON output file whose filename is listed in the reproducibility ledger (Appendix A). Reproduction command: python <script> from the repository root with no arguments. All random seeds are fixed at 42. Expected runtime per experiment: under 4 minutes on a consumer CPU.

4The Conservation Law: Three Physical Instantiations

4.1 The Two-Directional Lie Functional

Definition 1 (Lie Functional)

For a differentiable map \(f: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^m\), define the lie functional \begin{equation} \mathcal{L}(f) \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{x \sim \mathcal{D}}\!\left[|\log|\det J(x)||\right] \end{equation} where \(J(x) = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\) is the Jacobian. For non-square \(J\), replace \(|\det J|\) with \(\prod_i \sigma_i\) (product of singular values). \(\mathcal{L}(f) = 0\) if and only if \(f\) is locally volume-preserving.

Theorem 1 (Calibrated Lie Detection)

For a contraction map \(f_\alpha(x) = \alpha x\) with \(\alpha \in (0,1]\), \begin{equation} \mathcal{L}(f_\alpha) = -\log \alpha = \log(1/\alpha). \end{equation} This is tight: the functional measures exactly the information loss in nats. By Landauer's principle, the minimum energy cost of committing this lie is \(k_B T \cdot \mathcal{L}(f_\alpha) / \ln 2\) joules per inference.

Proof sketch. The Jacobian of \(f_\alpha(x) = \alpha x\) on \(\mathbb{R}^n\) is \(J = \alpha I_n\), giving \(|\det J| = \alpha^n\). For the scalar case (\(n=1\)): \(|\det J| = \alpha\), so \(\mathcal{L}(f_\alpha) = |\log \alpha| = -\log \alpha\) (since \(\alpha \leq 1\)). The expectation over \(\mathcal{D}\) is trivial as \(J\) is constant. Tightness follows because \(f_\alpha\) compresses each dimension by exactly factor \(\alpha\), destroying \(-\log \alpha\) nats of information per dimension. The Landauer bound follows directly by substituting into \(E_{\min} = k_B T \cdot \mathcal{L} / \ln 2\). \(\square\)

Definition 2 (Two-Directional Extension)

The one-directional functional \(\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{del}}(f) = \mathbb{E}[\max(0, -\log|\det J|)]\) catches only deletion lies (\(|\det J| < 1\)). The two-directional complete functional is \begin{equation} \mathcal{L}_2(f) \;=\; \mathbb{E}\!\left[|\log|\det J(x)||\right] \;=\; \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{del}}(f) + \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{fab}}(f) \end{equation} where \(\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{fab}} = \mathbb{E}[\max(0, +\log|\det J|)]\) catches fabrication lies.

Theorem 2 (Two-Directional Completeness)

The one-directional functional \(\mathcal{L}_1\) satisfies \(\mathcal{L}_1(\tilde f) = 0\) for any fabrication map \(\tilde f\) with \(|\det J| > 1\), certifying it as honest despite active hallucination. The two-directional \(\mathcal{L}_2\) satisfies \(\mathcal{L}_2(\tilde f) = \log|\det J| > 0\). Experimentally: fabrication caught at \(\mathcal{L}_2 = 1.0986\) nats (predicted: \(\ln 3 = 1.0986\)).

Proof sketch. Let \(\tilde f(x) = \beta x\) with \(\beta > 1\) (expansion map). Then \(|\det J| = \beta > 1\), so \(\log|\det J| = \log \beta > 0\). The deletion functional \(\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{del}} = \mathbb{E}[\max(0, -\log \beta)] = 0\) (since \(-\log \beta < 0\) and the max clips it). Thus \(\mathcal{L}_1(\tilde f) = 0\): the one-directional functional is blind to expansions. The two-directional functional evaluates \(\mathcal{L}_2 = \mathbb{E}[|\log \beta|] = \log \beta > 0\). For \(\beta = 3\): \(\mathcal{L}_2 = \ln 3 = 1.0986\), matching the experimental measurement exactly. The general case with non-constant \(J(x)\) follows by Jensen's inequality over the distribution \(\mathcal{D}\). \(\square\)

4.2 Quantum No-Hiding Enforcement

Theorem 3 (Quantum No-Hiding Certificate)

Let a quantum channel \(\Phi: \mathcal{H}_S \to \mathcal{H}_E\) act on state \(\rho_S\). By the no-hiding theorem (Braunstein & Pati, 2007), information deleted from the system must appear in the environment: \begin{equation} S(\rho_S) = S(\rho_E). \end{equation} A faithful quantum map achieves \(S(\rho_E) = 0\). Any deletion of information from the system is perfectly mirrored in the environment, enabling detection without inspecting model output. Measured gap: \(|S_{\mathrm{sys}} - S_{\mathrm{env}}| = 1.01 \times 10^{-13}\).

Proof sketch. This is a direct application of Braunstein & Pati (2007). For a composite system \(\mathcal{H}_S \otimes \mathcal{H}_E\) starting in a pure state, the Schmidt decomposition guarantees \(S(\rho_S) = S(\rho_E)\) for any bipartition. If the channel \(\Phi\) on \(\mathcal{H}_S\) is information-preserving (unitary), then \(\rho_E\) remains in its initial pure state, giving \(S(\rho_E) = 0\). If \(\Phi\) destroys information (maps \(\rho_S\) to a mixed state), then \(S(\rho_S) > 0\) and the no-hiding theorem guarantees \(S(\rho_E) = S(\rho_S)\): the deleted information has leaked to the environment. Our implementation uses \(4 \times 4\) density matrices in exact NumPy float64 arithmetic. The measured gap \(1.01 \times 10^{-13}\) is within density-matrix numerical precision for a 4-dimensional Hilbert space. \(\square\)

Verified gap: \(|S_{\mathrm{sys}} - S_{\mathrm{env}}| = 1.01 \times 10^{-13}\) (within density-matrix numerical precision).

5The Gauge Correction: Why \(|\det J|=1\) Is Not Enough

Critical Correction

Earlier work by the same author (Srivastava, 2026 [12]) stated: "A model is faithful if and only if \(|\det J| = 1\)." This biconditional is false. The condition \(|\det J| = 1\) is necessary but not sufficient for faithful attribution. We correct it here with a constructive counterexample (the gauge-twin) and experimental verification. The one-directional functional \(\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{det}}\) is also corrected to the two-directional \(\mathcal{L}_2\) to catch fabrication lies.

4.1 The Gauge-Twin Attack

Construct a gauge-twin: a network with identical predictions, identical reconstruction certificates, and \(|\det J| = 1\) — but with attribution weights permuted via an orthogonal transformation \(Q\) (where \(QQ^\top = I\)). Since \(|\det Q| = 1\), every determinant test passes. Yet the attribution story changes completely.

Theorem 4 (Gauge-Twin Indistinguishability)

For any faithful network \(f\) with attribution vector \(a(x)\), define the gauge-twin \(\tilde f = Q \circ f\) for orthogonal \(Q\). Then: \begin{align} \tilde f(x) &\equiv f(x) \quad(\text{gap} = 1.28 \times 10^{-14}), \\ \tilde f^{-1}(\tilde f(x)) &\equiv x \quad(\text{gap} = 1.37 \times 10^{-14}), \\ |\det J_{\tilde f}(x)| &= |\det Q| \cdot |\det J_f(x)| = 1, \\ \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{det}}(\tilde f) &= 4.44 \times 10^{-16} \approx 0. \end{align} Yet: \(\|\tilde a(x) - a(x)\| / \|a(x)\| = \mathbf{16.89\times}\) (1689% relative attribution swing).

Proof sketch. Let \(f\) be invertible with \(|\det J_f| = 1\). Define \(\tilde f = Q \circ f\) for any orthogonal \(Q \in O(n)\). (i) Prediction invariance: if we apply \(Q^{-1} = Q^\top\) at readout, \(\tilde f(x) = Q f(x)\), so predictions are related by an orthogonal transformation and are numerically indistinguishable at the output layer. (ii) Determinant invariance: \(J_{\tilde f} = Q J_f\), so \(|\det J_{\tilde f}| = |\det Q| \cdot |\det J_f| = 1 \cdot 1 = 1\) by the multiplicativity of determinants and \(|\det Q| = 1\) for orthogonal \(Q\). (iii) Attribution swing: the Jacobian-based attribution \(a(x) = J_f(x)^\top e\) transforms to \(\tilde a(x) = J_{\tilde f}(x)^\top e = J_f(x)^\top Q^\top e\). For generic \(Q\) and \(e\), \(\|Q^\top e - e\| / \|e\|\) can be \(O(1)\), giving arbitrarily large relative attribution swings. Experimentally: a specific \(Q\) is constructed to maximize this swing; the measured ratio is 16.89×. \(\square\)

Definition 3 (Attribution-Lie Functional)

The attribution-lie functional is \begin{equation} \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{attr}}(f) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} |\log \sigma_i(J(x))| \end{equation} where \(\sigma_i\) are the singular values of the Jacobian. Unlike \(\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{det}}\), this functional is sensitive to per-feature volume changes and catches orthogonal permutations of attributions.

Theorem 5 (Gauge Repair)

The attribution-lie functional detects the gauge-twin attack: \(\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{attr}}(\tilde f) = 5.163\) nats while \(\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{det}}(\tilde f) \approx 0\). The additive diagonal repair — replacing \(J\) with \(J + \epsilon D\) where \(D\) is the unique diagonal matrix minimizing \(\|J + D - I\|_F\) — restores uniqueness to \(9.2 \times 10^{-17}\).

Proof sketch. (i) Detection: \(\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{attr}}(f) = \sum_i |\log \sigma_i(J)|\) sums absolute log singular values. For orthogonal \(Q\), \(J_{\tilde f} = Q J_f\). While \(\prod_i \sigma_i = |\det J| = 1\) (so \(\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{det}} = 0\)), the individual singular values \(\sigma_i(QJ_f) \neq 1\) in general, giving \(\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{attr}} > 0\). The attribution-lie functional is sensitive to per-axis volume changes that cancel in the determinant. (ii) Uniqueness: given that \(|\det J| = 1\) and all singular values equal 1 (i.e., \(\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{attr}} = 0\)), \(J\) must be orthogonal. Among orthogonal Jacobians, the additive diagonal repair selects the unique one closest (in Frobenius norm) to the identity, breaking the gauge degeneracy. The repair residual of \(9.2 \times 10^{-17}\) is within float64 machine epsilon for a \(4 \times 4\) matrix. \(\square\)

6The NTK Identity

Definition 4 (Training-Support Concentration)

Define the training-support concentration of point \(x\) as \begin{equation} s(x)^2 = \frac{k(x,X) K^{-1} k(X,x)}{k(x,x)} \end{equation} where \(k(x,x') = K_{\mathrm{NTK}}(x,x')\) and \(K = k(X,X)\) for training set \(X\). We have \(s(x) \in [0,1]\): values near 1 indicate strong support from training data; near 0 indicates a gap.

Theorem 6 (NTK Identity)

In the NTK regime (infinite-width linearization), training-support concentration equals normalized NTK–GP posterior variance: \begin{equation} \boxed{s(x)^2 \;=\; 1 \;-\; \frac{\mathrm{Var}_{\mathrm{NTK}}(x)}{\|\nabla_\theta f(x)\|^2}} \end{equation} Empirical verification over \(n = 300\) independent networks: identity error \(= 3.27 \times 10^{-13}\), AUC \(= 0.859 \pm 0.084\).

Proof sketch. In the NTK-GP regime, the model function is a Gaussian process with kernel \(k(x,x') = K_{\mathrm{NTK}}(x,x')\). The NTK-GP posterior variance at \(x\) is \(\mathrm{Var}_{\mathrm{NTK}}(x) = k(x,x) - k(x,X) K^{-1} k(X,x)\) where \(K = k(X,X)\). Note that \(k(x,x) = \|\nabla_\theta f(x)\|^2\) (the squared gradient norm). Dividing: \(\mathrm{Var}_{\mathrm{NTK}}(x) / k(x,x) = 1 - k(x,X) K^{-1} k(X,x) / k(x,x) = 1 - s(x)^2\) by Definition 4, which rearranges to the claimed identity. The derivation is exact in the infinite-width limit; the empirical error of \(3.27 \times 10^{-13}\) over finite-width networks reflects linearization error, not a failure of the identity itself. \(\square\)

□

The NTK identity establishes the H-axis signal precisely: the NTK–GP posterior variance, normalized by the gradient norm, is the unique information-theoretically optimal reference-free signal for detecting data gaps. It detects where the model might be wrong, not how wrong.

7The Detection Ceiling

Lemma 1 (A-Independence of Internal Signals)

Let the confabulation magnitude \(A\) be defined as a label shift: for an honest sample \((x, y)\), the confabulated version is \((x, y + A)\). For any measurable function \(\phi: (\mathrm{model}, x) \to \mathbb{R}\) that depends only on the model parameters \(\theta\) and input \(x\) (not on any label or output), \(\phi(\mathrm{model}, x; A) = \phi(\mathrm{model}, x; 0)\) for all \(A \in \mathbb{R}\) and all \(x\).

Proof. By hypothesis, \(\phi\) is a function of \((\theta, x)\) only. The confabulation parameter \(A\) appears exclusively in the label \(y + A\), which does not enter \(\phi\). Therefore \(\partial \phi / \partial A = 0\) identically. \(\square\)

Theorem 7 (Detection Ceiling)

For any measurable function \(\phi(\text{model}, x)\) that depends only on model internals and input \(x\), and for any confabulation magnitude parameter \(A\), \begin{equation} \frac{\partial \phi(\text{model}, x)}{\partial A} = 0. \end{equation} Consequently, the flag-rate \(v = \Pr[\phi(x) > \tau]\) is constant in \(A\): \begin{equation} \frac{\partial v}{\partial A} = 0 \quad \forall \tau \in \mathbb{R}. \end{equation} While precision \(\Pr[\phi(x) > \tau \mid x \text{ is a lie}]\) can range over \([0,1]\) as \(A\) varies, the flag-rate cannot move. Any single-model signal that flags more lies also flags proportionally more honest outputs.

Proof sketch. By Lemma 1, \(\phi(\mathrm{model},x)\) is constant in \(A\). Therefore the distribution of \(\phi\) over any fixed population of inputs \(x \sim \mathcal{D}\) is also constant in \(A\), and \(v(\tau, A) = \Pr_{x \sim \mathcal{D}}[\phi(x) > \tau] = v(\tau, 0)\) for all \(A\). The flag-rate \(v\) is a property of the detector and the input distribution, not of the labels. Precision, by contrast, is \(\Pr[\phi(x) > \tau \mid x \text{ is a lie}] = v / \Pr[x \text{ is a lie}]\), and as \(A\) increases, larger lies are easier to sample into the flagged set (because they tend to fall in low-support regions), so precision can increase — but the denominator \(v\) does not move. Formally: \(\partial v / \partial A = \int (\partial / \partial A) \mathbf{1}[\phi(x) > \tau] \, d\mathcal{D}(x) = \int 0 \, d\mathcal{D}(x) = 0\). \(\square\)

□
Corollary 1

9The Minimal Complete Certificate

Theorem 8 (H + G Completeness)

Define the two-axis certificate: \begin{equation} \mathrm{H}(x) = \frac{\mathrm{Var}_{\mathrm{NTK}}(x)}{\|\nabla_\theta f(x)\|^2} \in [0,1] \end{equation} \begin{equation} \mathrm{G}(x) = \|f(x) - g(x)\|_2 \end{equation} where \(g(x)\) is the externally grounded value for input \(x\). Then H + G is:

  • Necessary: H alone has ceiling \(\partial v/\partial A = 0\). G alone requires ground truth unavailable in deployment.
  • Sufficient: H detects the gap (exposure); G quantifies the lie (magnitude). Together they determine whether confabulation occurred and how severely.
  • Minimal: No certificate using fewer than two signals can be simultaneously gap-detecting and magnitude-calibrated.

Proof sketch. (i) Necessity of H: by Theorem 7, no single-model signal \(\phi\) can supply magnitude information. The NTK posterior variance (Theorem 6) is the unique information-theoretically optimal signal for gap detection, so H is the best available internal signal. (ii) Necessity of G: H detects \(s(x)\) (whether \(x\) is in the training support) but is independent of \(y + A\) by Lemma 1. A second independent signal providing \(\|f(x) - g(x)\|\) is required to access magnitude. (iii) Sufficiency: \(\mathrm{H}(x) > h_0\) flags in-distribution gaps; \(\mathrm{G}(x) > g_0\) flags magnitude violations. Their conjunction covers both failure modes. (iv) Minimality: removing H leaves magnitude detection only (misses in-distribution gaps); removing G leaves gap detection only (ceiling applies). No sub-certificate of size 1 is sufficient by the above. \(\square\)

□

The H + G certificate derives the structure of frontier AI verification pipelines from first principles: an internal uncertainty signal routes to an external verifier call, which supplies the G-axis. The routing threshold is optimized by setting \(\mathrm{H}(x) > h_0\) for the deployment-specific cost of a false negative.

Figure 8. The minimal complete certificate pipeline. The model computes the H-axis (NTK posterior variance, normalized) from its internal gradients. If \(\mathrm{H}(x) > h_0\), a data gap is detected and an external verifier oracle is called to supply the G-axis magnitude. If \(\mathrm{H}(x) \leq h_0\), the input is in-support and the model routes without an external call. Theorem 7 proves H alone cannot supply G — routing to an external call is irreducible, not an engineering convenience.

12Scope and Limitations

Biconditional corrected. "\(|\det J| = 1 \Leftrightarrow\) faithful" was false. Corrected: necessary, not sufficient. \(\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{attr}}\) is the completing condition.

NTK identity is numerically fragile on near-singular kernels. On real 11-D data, identity error grows to \(1.90 \times 10^3\). The analytic proof is exact; implementation requires auto-scaled ridge regularization for high-dimensional real data.

Transformer experiment is an analog, not exact replication. The attention-KL used here is \(\mathrm{KL}(\alpha^{(l,h)} \| \mathcal{U})\) — same family as van Dijk (2026) but not the exact implementation. The ceiling result holds for any such signal by Theorem 7.

Quantum experiments are density-matrix simulations. The no-hiding result is computed in exact NumPy density-matrix arithmetic. Real QPU results include hardware noise but the qualitative result is noise-robust.

G-axis requires ground truth. The two-axis certificate is complete for evaluation-time auditing. Live deployment without a verifier cannot use G. The ceiling applies.

Semantic lies remain out of scope. A model that answers a different question while remaining numerically close to some truth evades all detectors presented here.

13Conclusion

We have unified two independent lines of investigation into one theorem. From the structural direction: model faithfulness is a conservation law, and \(|\det J| = 1\) plus the additive diagonal gauge defines the complete structural certificate. From the detection direction: the best single-model reference-free signal is the NTK–GP posterior variance, and it has a provable ceiling (\(\partial v/\partial A = 0\)) that prevents calibration for in-distribution confabulation.

The bridge is the NTK identity: the information component H is exactly \(\mathrm{Var}_{\mathrm{NTK}}/\|\nabla_\theta f\|^2\). H tells you where the model might be wrong. G (grounding) tells you how wrong. H + G is the minimal complete certificate.

The One-Sentence Result

Single-model, reference-free, in-distribution hallucination detection is provably uncalibratable — the best internal signal detects gaps, not lies — and the lie/no-lie distinction inside a gap is information that only external grounding can supply.

The Open Problem

Does the ceiling extend to multi-sample signals? Semantic entropy (Farquhar et al., 2024) accesses magnitude information through variance across multiple completions — this is not \(\phi(\mathrm{model}, x)\) for a single \(x\), because the inter-sample variance is a function of multiple outputs and thus may carry magnitude information that a single-pass signal cannot. If the ceiling is purely a single-sample phenomenon, multi-sample signals should show flag-rate that grows with \(A\).

Falsifiable Experiment (Future Work)

Protocol: Run semantic entropy (Farquhar et al., 2024) through the same A-sweep used in §§7–8: synthetic networks, \(n = 300\), confabulation magnitude \(A \in \{0, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, 4.0, 8.0\}\), same threshold grid. Measure flag-rate standard deviation across A values.

Outcomes: (a) If \(\partial v_{\mathrm{sem}}/\partial A = 0\) (flag-rate std = 0), the ceiling is deeper than proven here and applies to any signal that averages over outputs of the same model. (b) If \(\partial v_{\mathrm{sem}}/\partial A > 0\) (flag-rate grows with A), multi-sample entropy escapes the ceiling and provides magnitude information — consistent with our Theorem 7 scope (single model, single pass). Results will be uploaded to the same Zenodo repository as semantic_entropy_sweep.json.
